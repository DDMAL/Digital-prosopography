Abel, Karl Friedrich, German viola da gamba player and composer; b. Cothen, Dec. 22, 1723; d. London, June 20, 1787. He studied with his father; then with J. S. Bach at the Thomasschule in Leipzig. He was a member of the Royal Polish Band at Dresden (17481759, he became a friend of John Christian Bach. With the Duke of York's assistance, he was appointed chamberQueen Charlotte (1765). He composed two operas: Love in a Village (London, 1760) and Berenice (London, 1764) ; several symphonies, many overtures, quartets and harpsichord sonatas. Abel is generally regarded as the last great virtuoso on the viola da gamba.
