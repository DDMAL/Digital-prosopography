Abbey, John, noted English organbuilder; b. Whilton, Northamptonshire, Dec. 22, 1785; d. Versailles, Feb. 19, 1859. He went to Paris in 1826 at the invitation of Sebastien Erard to construct an organ for the Paris Exposition. He remained in France and built organs for the cathedrals of many French cities. In 1831 he installed an organ at the Paris Opera. His innovations in the English type of bellows were adopted by many French organ builders. His sons, E. and J. Abbey, inherited the business, situated at Versailles.
