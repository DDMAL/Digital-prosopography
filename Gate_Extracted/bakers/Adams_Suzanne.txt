Adams, Suzanne, American soprano; b. Cambridge, Mass., Nov. 28, 1872; d. London, Feb. 5, 1953. She studied with J. Bouhy in New York; went to Paris in 1889; made her debut at the Paris Opera as Juliette (Jan. 9, 1895), and subsequently was engaged to sing there for 3 more seasons; appeared at the Metropolitan Opera House as Juliette on Jan. 4, 1899.
