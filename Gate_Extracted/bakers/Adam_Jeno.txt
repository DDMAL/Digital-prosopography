Adam, Jeno, Hungarian conductor, composer and writer on music; b. Szigetszentmiklos, Dec. 13, 1896. He studied with Kodaly, and was later associated with him in a program reorganizing the system of musical education; conducted several choirs in Budapest, and in 1938 was appointed prof, of choral singing at the Academy of Music there. He has written 2 operas, Hungarian Christmas (Budapest, Dec. 22, 1931) and Maria Veronika (Budapest, 1938); a symph. cantata Man on the Road (1946); 2 string quartets; a cello sonata and unaccompanied choral works. He has also published a number of musical text books for schools.
