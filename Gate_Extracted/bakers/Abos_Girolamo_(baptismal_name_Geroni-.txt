Abos, Girolamo (baptismal name Geronimo), Maltese composer; b. Valetta, Nov. 16, 1715; d. Naples, May, 1760. He studied with Leonardo Leo and Francesco Durante in Naples. In 1756 he went to London as 'maestro al cembalo' at the Italian Theater. Returning to Naples in 1758 he taught at the Cons, della Pieta de' Turchini. Among his pupils was Paisiello. Abos _ wrote 14 operas which were produced in Naples, Rome and London; of these, Tito Manlio (Naples, May 30, 1751) was successful; also composed 7 masses and other church music.
