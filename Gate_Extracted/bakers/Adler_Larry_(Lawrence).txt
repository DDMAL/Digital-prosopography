Adler, Larry (Lawrence), harmonica player; b. Baltimore, Feb. 10, 1914. He won a harmonica contest at 13 for the best rendition of Beethoven's Minuet in G. He has appeared in numerous revues; also gave concerts as soloist with piano and with orchestra, as well as command performances for King George VI, King Gustav of Sweden and Presidents Roosevelt and Truman. In 1940, determined to learn to read music, he took lessons with Ernst Toch. Darius Milhaud wrote a Suite for harmonica and orch. for Adler, which he played on Nov. 16, 1945 with the Philadelphia Orch.
