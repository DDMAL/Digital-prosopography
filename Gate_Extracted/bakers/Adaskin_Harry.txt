Adaskin, Harry, Canadian violinist; b. Riga, Latvia, Sept. 17, 1901. He was brought to Toronto at the age of two, and studied at the Toronto Cons. (1912then entered the Chicago Musical College and later studied in Paris. From 1923he was second violinist of the Hart House String Quartet, which toured in Europe and America. In 1946 he was appointed violin teacher at the Univ. of British Columbia, Vancouver. His brother, Murray Adaskin (b. Toronto, March 28, 1906) is also a violinist; another brother, John Adaskin (b. Toronto, June 4, 1908) is a cellist.
