Abraham, Otto, German specialist in tone psychology; b. Berlin, May 31, 1872; d. there, Jan. 24, 1926. He studied medicine; became an associate of Stumpf at the Berlin Psychological Institute from 1894; then collaborated with Hornbostel in building up the Archive of Phonographic Recordings in Berlin. He published several valuable treatises on acoustics and primitive music, among them W ahrnehmung kiirzester Tone und Gerausche (1898); Studien ilber das Tonsystem und die Musik der Japaner (1904); Phono graphierte Indianermelodien aus Britischbostel). He also wrote studies on recorded Turkish, Siamese and Hindu melodies; a paper on Chinese musical notation, etc.
