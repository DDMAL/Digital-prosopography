Adler, Clarence, American pianist; b. Cincinnati, March 10, 1886. He studied at the Cincinnati College of Music (18981904); then in Berlin with Godowsky (1905in the Hekking Trio. Returning to America in 1913, he settled in New York; made his American debut with the N.Y. Symph. Orch. (Feb. 8, 1914). In 1941 he broadcast all of Mozart's 28 piano concertos. He has published an album of piano pieces; also arrangements of works by Dvorak and Franck.
