Aaron, abbot of the monasteries of St. Martin and St. Pantaleon at Cologne, where he died on Dec. 14, 1052. He was the author of two historically important treatises: De tit Hit ate cantus vocalis et de modo cantandi atque psallendi and De regulis tonorum et symphoniarum. He is believed to be the first cleric to introduce the Gregorian evening service (nocturns) into Germany.
