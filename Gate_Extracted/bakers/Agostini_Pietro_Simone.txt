Agostini, Pietro Simone, Italian composer; b. Rome, c. 1650. He was in the service of the Duke of Parma as maestro di cappella. He wrote 6 operas: Tolemeo (Venice, 1668) ; Ippolita (Milan, 1670) ; La costanza di Rosmonda (Genoa, 1670) ; Adelinda (Aricia, 1673); 7/ Ratto delle Sabine (Venice, 1680); and Floridea (Venice, 1687). Some of his operas were written in collaboration with Busca, Ziani and others. He also wrote oratorios, motets and secular cantatas.
