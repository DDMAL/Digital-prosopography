Abraham, Max, German publisher; b. Danzig, June 3, 1831; d. Leipzig, Dec. 8, 1900. He became a partner in G. F. Peters' 'Bureau de Musique' in 1863, and sole proprietor in 1880. On Jan. 1, 1894, his nephew, Heinrich Hinrichsen, of Hamburg, entered the firm and, upon Abraham's death, became its head. The famous 'Edition Peters' was inaugurated by Abraham.
